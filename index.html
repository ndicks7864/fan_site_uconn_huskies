Lady Basketball Huskies

The Comeback

Last year in the final game of the NCAA Final Four, the Uconn Huskies lost their 111 game streak to the Mississippi Bulldogs. Going into overtime, they lost the game 66-64 when Bulldog, Morgan William, made the winning shot at the buzzer in overtime. Coach Geno Auriemma stating after the game that “there are things you can’t pretend didn’t happen”. The team feelings something they had not felt since 2014, the feelings of loss. Well known player Katie Lou Samuelson saying that “We [the team] just have to learn from it and we want to have a fresh start, not to get too caught up on the past.” Uconn has high intentions going forward and doesn’t plan to let them down anytime soon. They’ve already started off right, opening the new 2017-2018 season with a 6-0 record. The Huskies are hungry to win and do not plan to stop eating until they have the full meal.

Katie Lou Samuelson's Foot Injury

Junior Katie Lou Samuelson, a key player one the Uconn Huskies women's basketball team, injuried herself in the NCAA Championship semi final agaisnt Oregon State. She broke her left foot in the first quarter of the game and at first did not realize it was break, but thought it was just a sprain. Being such a key player to the Uconn Huskies team, coaches would love to see her up and playing again; they realize that her health comes first and intend on letting her foot heal complety before playing again. Katie Lou's high school coach, Kevin Kiernan, speaking on the topic saying that "as a coach, you think the worst... But thankfully that's not the case and she'll hopefully just miss a few games." Coaches say she will be out for the next three games at minimum and that she will recieve weekly evaluations.
